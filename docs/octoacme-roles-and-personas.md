# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria clarification
- Participate in knowledge sharing and mentoring

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability
- Contribute to technical excellence and team learning

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Clarifies acceptance criteria and feature requirements
- **Project Manager**: Reports progress and blockers in standups and syncs
- **Tech Lead**: Receives design guidance, participates in code reviews
- **QA Lead**: Collaborates on test planning and receives quality feedback
- **DevOps Engineer**: Works with on deployment and environment setup
- **Security Lead**: Addresses security feedback in code reviews

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed
- Collaboration on acceptance criteria and test cases

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics
- Create and refine acceptance criteria
- Participate in planning and review ceremonies
- Ensure customer voice is heard in decisions

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability
- Build products customers love

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Syncs weekly on progress and blockers; makes scope/timeline trade-offs
- **Developers**: Collaborates on feasibility and acceptance criteria clarity
- **Tech Lead**: Consults on technical feasibility and trade-offs
- **QA Lead**: Reviews acceptance criteria and test strategy
- **Sponsor / Stakeholders**: Aligns on priorities and decisions
- **Security Lead**: Reviews security requirements and compliance needs

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement
- Acceptance criteria creation and acceptance testing

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication
- Track progress metrics and velocity
- Escalate issues appropriately
- Protect team from external interruptions

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders
- Build trust and predictability

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Daily alignment on priorities and scope
- **Tech Lead**: Escalation point for technical blockers and risks
- **QA Lead**: Tracks quality metrics and release readiness
- **DevOps Engineer**: Coordinates deployment planning and readiness
- **Sponsor / Stakeholders**: Provides weekly status and escalations
- **All team members**: Available for daily support and blocker resolution

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation
- One-on-ones with team members on blockers

---

## QA / Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads define and execute testing strategies to ensure deliverables meet quality standards and acceptance criteria. They collaborate with product, engineering, and project leads to validate that features work as intended and identify defects early.

### Responsibilities
- Develop comprehensive test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Design and execute unit, integration, and end-to-end tests
- Validate release readiness through smoke tests and UAT
- Report quality metrics and defects with clear severity levels
- Partner with developers on test automation and CI integration
- Identify quality risks during planning and execution phases
- Participate in acceptance criteria refinement
- Track test coverage and quality trends

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality features that meet or exceed customer expectations
- Reduce production defects and rework cycles
- Enable confident, rapid releases
- Shift quality left through early testing and collaboration

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Clarifies acceptance criteria and definition of done
- **Developers**: Collaborates on testability, test automation, and defect triage
- **Project Manager**: Reports quality metrics and release readiness status
- **Tech Lead**: Discusses test strategy and quality risks
- **DevOps Engineer**: Coordinates staging environment setup and monitoring
- **Security Lead**: Collaborates on security testing approach

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and review meetings
- Daily standups focused on test progress and blockers
- Quality reports in weekly stakeholder updates
- Pre-release quality sign-off
- Defect triage meetings and severity discussions

## Technical Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide technical direction, make architectural decisions, and guide the engineering team on best practices. They ensure solutions are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with platform standards.

### Responsibilities
- Review technical designs and architecture proposals
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigation strategies
- Guide code reviews and establish coding standards
- Mentor junior developers and promote technical growth
- Lead technical discussions during planning and design phases
- Ensure compliance with security, performance, and operational standards
- Participate in acceptance criteria from technical perspective
- Lead or participate in spike work for complex problems

### Goals
- Build scalable, maintainable solutions
- Reduce technical debt and rework
- Foster a culture of technical excellence
- Enable team growth and knowledge sharing

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Escalates technical risks and dependencies
- **Developers**: Provides design guidance and code review feedback
- **Product Manager**: Advises on technical feasibility and trade-offs
- **DevOps Engineer**: Collaborates on scalability and operational requirements
- **QA Lead**: Discusses test strategy and quality risks
- **Security Lead**: Partners on security architecture and compliance

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture discussions
- Code review comments and merge approvals
- Risk register updates for technical blockers
- Weekly technical sync meetings
- Mentoring and knowledge sharing sessions

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove process impediments, and coach the team on continuous improvement. They ensure the team follows agreed-upon processes while fostering psychological safety and collaboration.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and help remove impediments blocking the team
- Coach the team on agile practices and principles
- Track process metrics (velocity, burndown) and share insights
- Facilitate conflict resolution and difficult conversations
- Protect the team from external interruptions during sprints
- Support continuous process improvement
- Build psychological safety and team cohesion

### Goals
- Enable sustainable, high-velocity delivery
- Build a psychologically safe, self-organizing team
- Continuously improve team processes and outcomes
- Foster learning and experimentation

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Partners on schedule and dependency management
- **Product Manager**: Protects sprint scope and prioritization
- **Developers**: Facilitates collaboration and removes blockers
- **Tech Lead**: Works together on impediment resolution
- **QA Lead**: Supports team dynamics and collaboration
- **All team members**: Coaches, facilitates, and removes impediments

### Typical Communication
- Sprint ceremonies and retrospectives
- One-on-ones with team members on process concerns
- Metrics and process improvement tracking
- Escalations to Project Manager for cross-team blockers
- Coaching and feedback on agile practices

## Stakeholder / Business Sponsor

### Role Summary
Business Sponsors provide business context, set priorities, and make resource and strategic decisions. They ensure projects align with business goals and represent customer and organizational interests.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives and success metrics
- Set and adjust project priorities
- Approve key milestones and release decisions
- Provide resources (budget, team capacity) as needed
- Escalate business risks and dependencies
- Review project status and provide steering feedback
- Participate in go/no-go release decisions
- Communicate project value to broader organization

### Goals
- Maximize business value and ROI of project investments
- Ensure alignment with organizational strategy
- Enable informed decision-making at critical junctures
- Support team success and remove barriers

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Receives status updates and escalations; approves key decisions
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on business objectives and success metrics
- **Team**: Provides context and removes resource constraints
- **Tech Lead**: Consulted on technical feasibility of strategic decisions

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates
- Milestone review and approval meetings
- Escalation communications for business risks
- Strategic alignment meetings
- Release sign-off and approval

## DevOps / Infrastructure Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers design, build, and maintain deployment infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and production systems. They enable reliable, scalable, and observable deployments.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automation
- Provision and manage deployment environments (staging, production)
- Establish monitoring, logging, and alerting for production systems
- Manage infrastructure security, backups, and disaster recovery
- Support incident response and rollback procedures
- Collaborate on performance and scalability optimization
- Participate in pre-release readiness reviews
- Document deployment procedures and runbooks

### Goals
- Enable rapid, reliable deployments with minimal risk
- Maintain high availability and performance of production systems
- Reduce deployment friction and enable self-service deployments
- Build observable and resilient infrastructure

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Tech Lead**: Collaborates on architecture and scalability decisions
- **Developers**: Supports debugging, deployment, and observability
- **Project Manager**: Reports on deployment readiness and infrastructure risks
- **QA Lead**: Provides staging environments and production monitoring data
- **Security Lead**: Partners on infrastructure security and compliance

### Typical Communication
- Deployment planning and execution meetings
- Infrastructure risk discussions during planning phases
- Incident response and post-mortems
- Observability and performance dashboards shared with team
- CI/CD pipeline status and improvements

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads ensure that projects incorporate security best practices, comply with organizational and regulatory standards, and identify and mitigate security risks.

### Responsibilities
- Review designs and implementations for security vulnerabilities
- Define security requirements and acceptance criteria
- Conduct or arrange security testing and code reviews
- Manage security incidents and coordinate incident response
- Maintain security compliance and audit readiness
- Educate team members on secure development practices
- Participate in risk identification and mitigation
- Review infrastructure and deployment security

### Goals
- Deliver secure features that protect customer and organizational data
- Reduce security vulnerabilities and compliance violations
- Build a security-conscious development culture
- Enable security without slowing delivery

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Tech Lead**: Collaborates on secure architecture and design
- **Developers**: Reviews code for security issues and provides guidance
- **Project Manager**: Escalates security risks and dependencies; updates risk register
- **DevOps Engineer**: Ensures secure infrastructure and access controls
- **QA Lead**: Coordinates security testing approach
- **Product Manager**: Advises on security requirements and privacy implications

### Typical Communication
- Security design reviews during planning phases
- Security-focused code review comments
- Security risk updates in risk register
- Incident response and post-mortem coordination
- Security training and awareness sessions

## How These Personas Are Used

1. **Project Staffing**: Use these definitions to identify which roles your project needs
2. **Onboarding**: Share relevant persona descriptions with new team members
3. **Clarifying Responsibilities**: Reference these when role boundaries become unclear
4. **Cross-functional Collaboration**: Understand how your role interacts with others
5. **Skills Exercise**: Use these as personas for scenario-based learning

---

## Notes on Specialization

- **Multiple Roles**: One person can hold multiple roles (e.g., PM + Scrum Master) for smaller projects
- **Specialized Expertise**: Tech Lead, QA Lead, DevOps, and Security roles are often specialized; consider dedicated resource needs
- **Part-time Roles**: Some roles (e.g., Scrum Master, Security Lead) may be part-time or shared across projects
- **Role Growth**: Use these definitions to identify development opportunities for team members
